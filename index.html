Welcome

Yogbhayananda Shri Madhavnath Maharaj was born on Saka 1779 Chaitra Shukla Pratipada, i.e. Thursday 26 March 1857, in a Deshastha Brahmin family of Mrs. Mathurabai and Mr. Malharda Ratnaparkhi (Kulkarni) at Pangari, a village in Karvi, district Chitrakoot, Uttar Pradesh.

Shri Madhavnath Maharaj attained spiritual enlightenment in the Balaji temple which contains the samadhi of Shri. Guptanath. After that, Shri Madhavnath Maharaj when thirteen years old went on a journey which would take him to Badri-Kedar, Rameshwaram, the twelve jyotirlingas and the samadhi sthaan of the Nava Natha's (nine masters).

Shri Madhavnath Maharaj spread the importance of Yoga and Namaskar all over India. He blessed his followers to build a Lakshmi-Venkatesh temple at Rangari in Devgaon, Aurangabad district. The Shrinath temple was then built at Thorgavahana in Jalgaon. Lakshmibai Halwai built the famous Dagdusheth Halwai Datta Temple at Budhwar Peth, Pune with the inspiration of Shri Madhavnath Maharaj.Read More...

Someone Smiles Today!

 heartwarming sentiment that encapsulates the power of kindness and positivity in our daily lives. It serves as a reminder of the impact small acts of kindness can have on others' well-being and the ripple effect of spreading joy.

In a world often filled with challenges and hardships, this phrase encourages us to be mindful of the simple yet profound ways we can make a difference. Whether it's offering a friendly gesture, a listening ear, or a genuine compliment, we all have the ability to brighten someone else's day.

By choosing to be the reason behind someone's smile, we contribute to a more compassionate and caring world. This phrase invites us to look beyond ourselves and recognize the beauty in uplifting others, fostering connections, and creating positive moments in the lives of those around us.
